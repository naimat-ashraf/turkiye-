About Turkey

Turkey (Türkiye) is a transcontinental country straddling Europe and Asia, known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning landscapes. It borders eight countries and is surrounded by the Aegean, Mediterranean, and Black Seas. Historically, Turkey was home to ancient civilizations and later the Ottoman Empire. The modern Republic of Turkey was founded in 1923 by Mustafa Kemal Atatürk. Turkish culture blends Eastern and Western traditions, seen in its cuisine, music, and arts. Major attractions include Istanbul’s Hagia Sophia, Cappadocia’s fairy chimneys, and the beaches of Antalya. Turkey has a diverse economy, strong in agriculture, industry, and tourism. Turkish is the official language, and the country is secular, with a majority Muslim population. Today, Turkey is a key player in regional and global politics, offering a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty.
