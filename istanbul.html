About Istanbul

Istanbul, Turkey’s largest city, spans two continents and is rich in history, culture, and architecture. From the majestic Hagia Sophia to the vibrant Grand Bazaar, Istanbul offers a unique blend of tradition and modernity. Visitors can enjoy delicious cuisine, historic landmarks, and breathtaking Bosphorus views in every corner of this incredible city.

History of Istanbul

660 BC – Foundation of Byzantium

The ancient city of Byzantium was founded by Greek settlers, marking the beginning of Istanbul’s rich history.

330 AD – Constantinople

Roman Emperor Constantine the Great declared the city as the new capital of the Roman Empire.

1453 – Ottoman Conquest

Sultan Mehmed II conquered Constantinople, making it the capital of the Ottoman Empire.

1923 – Part of Modern Türkiye

After the fall of the Ottoman Empire, Istanbul became a major cultural and economic center of Türkiye.
